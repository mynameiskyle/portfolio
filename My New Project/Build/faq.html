Frequently Asked Questions

  • How does Dealerexit make money?

    The buyer decides how we're paid. A buyer can pay a finder's fee per rooftop of $10,000 out of closing proceeds when a deal closes due to our introduction. Or a buyer can pay $500 upfront when a seller accepts an invitation to talk from a buyer. It's the buyer's decision. Either way, when a broker is involved, the broker usually agrees with us to cover anything due to us out of his commission. It's always free for a seller to list on Dealerexit.

  • What do buyers see on Dealerexit?

    We know that anonymity is critical in the buy-sell process. As a seller, once word leaks that you're entertaining an offer, the rumor mill runs rampant, and your best people start to leave the store. With every departure, your blue sky drops. On Dealerexit, buyers won't see a seller's name or location. Instead, they'll see listings of anonymized dealerships for sale listing a few attributes. For example, a buyer would see "Chevrolet in a Southwestern state." If the buyer is interested, the buyer asks us to connect him with the seller. Then we ask the seller if he's willing to disclose the store's identity to this buyer. If so, then we connect the two parties.
